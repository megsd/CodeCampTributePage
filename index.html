Temple Grandin

Livestock Facilities Designer,
Animal Science PhD, Author, Speaker

About Temple Grandin

  • Born in Boston, MA in 1947
  • Diagnosed with autism at the age of 2
  • At age 15, she met science teacher William Carlock who became her mentor
  • Grandin invented and built a "hug box" at the age of 18 with Carlock's support and encouragment
  • By 1989, Grandin had earned a B.S. in Human Psychology, and a PhD in Animal Science
  • In 1980, she published her first two papers on beef cattle behavior during handling: "Livestock Behavior as Related to Handling Facilities Design"
  • In 1993, she edited the first edition of Livestock Handling in Transport
  • In the 1990's Grandin developed a restraining system for cattle during stunning in large beef plants
  • Grandin developed an objective numerical scoring system for assesing animal welfare at slaughter plants
  • Grandin developed curved corrals designed to reduce stress in animals being led to slaughter
  • In 2010, Time named her as one of the 100 most influential people
  • For more information, visit Wikipedia
